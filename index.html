Will Holdsworth vs Hilton Car Supermarket
SEAT Leon (AF70YMC) Rejection Case

Case Summary

Will Holdsworth officially rejected SEAT Leon AF70YMC on September 16, 2025, citing unsatisfactory quality under the Consumer Rights Act 2015. The rejection was based on non-functioning front/side parking sensors and a glitching cockpit screen that were not disclosed at point of sale. The vehicle was not driven immediately after purchase due to Will using a hire car provided by Auxillis until August 26, 2025, explaining the lack of evidence prior to August 24, 2025, as the SEAT was on Will's driveway.

Key Evidence: Non-functioning sensors (PXL_20250913_100916384.jpg), glitching cockpit screen (PXL_20250904_201118138~2.mp4), and Hilton diagnostic photograph confirming parking system fault (20250916_110043[1].jpg).

Formal Communications: Initial rejection letter sent September 16, 2025 (9:36 AM, Re_AF70YMC.eml), followed by formal letter before court claim on September 18, 2025 (5:59 PM, Re_AF70YMC (1).eml), demanding a full refund of £11,848.
